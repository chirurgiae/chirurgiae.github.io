Eponyms in Surgery

Amyand's Hernia

Amyand's hernia is the incarceration of appendix in an inguinal hernia. It is named after an English surgeon, Claudius Amyand, who performed the first successful appendectomy in 1735.

De Garengeot's Hernia

De Garengeot's hernia is the incarceration of the vermiform appendix within a femoral hernia. It is named after the French surgeon Croissant de Garengeot who described it in 1731.

Did you know ?

Historically, the term "operating theatre" referred to a non-sterile, tiered theater or amphitheater in which students and other spectators could watch surgeons perform surgery.
Artist's depiction of the old "operating theatre"

Regulations for the Theatre

'Apprentices and the Dressers of the Surgeon who operates are to stand round the Table. The Dressers of the other Surgeons are to occupy the three front Rows. The Surgeon’s pupils are to take their Places in the Rows above. Visitors are admitted by permission of the Surgeon.'

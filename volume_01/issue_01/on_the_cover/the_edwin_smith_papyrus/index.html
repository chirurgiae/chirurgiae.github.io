The Edwin Smith Papyrus

The Edwin Smith Papyrus is an Ancient Egyptian medical text dating from the 17th century B.C., the oldest known surgical treatise, & one of the oldest of all known medical papyri. It contains 48 systematically arranged case histories, mainly on trauma divided into head and neck, thorax, spine etc. Each presentation of a case is divided into title, examination, diagnosis, and treatment. Mostly employed are rational surgical treatments rather than medico-’magical’ measures widely prevalent during those times. It contains the first descriptions of the cranial sutures, the meninges, the external surface of the brain, the CSF etc. It also contains the first accounts of surgical stitching and of various types of dressings. It is currently housed at the New York Academy of Medicine.

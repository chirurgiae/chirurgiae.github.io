Perspective Article

Diabetic Gangrene is not Heaven-Sent, but Earth-Born !!!

Dr. Rajendran M C, Assistant Professor

Dept. of Surgery, Medical College Hospital, Thiruvananthapuram

The estimated lifetime risk of a foot ulcer for patients with diabetes (type 1 or 2) may be as high as 25 percent [1-3]. The major risk factors include neuropathy, arteriopathy, foot deformity, poorly controlled diabetes (high HbA1C), smoking and a past history of prior foot ulceration. [4] A major non biological risk factor resulting in amputation is poverty leading to inaccessibility to medical/surgical care.

The pathogenesis of diabetic foot ulceration starts from within. The loss of sensation due to neuropathy, superadded with ischemia and foot result in ulceration.(Fig.1&2) Later, the infection spreads from the local site, through the tissue planes proximally and distally resulting in limb threatening or even life threatening situations.

Avoid:

  • Bare foot walking - Increases chances of inadvertent trauma.
  • Bathroom surgery like cutting of ingrown nail using a razor.
  • Edible oils for foot massage - which can lead to ant bites!
  • Poorly fitted footwear esp. hard slippers - neuropathy commonly affects the first web space!
  • Strategies recommended to reduce these complications include :
  • Good control of Diabetes
  • Appropriate antibiotics as per the culture and sensitivity
  • Adequate wound debridement (Merciless cutting!)
  • Pressure offloading/ Redistribution of pressure from ulcer site.
  • Measures to improve vascularity - Revascularization procedures.
  • Addressing co-morbidities - like Coronary artery disease, Nephropathy.
  • Protective footwear esp. in a high risk foot [those with neuropathy, arteriopathy and/or deformity]. [5]

A team approach to diabetes care, including patient education and podiatric care, could prevent or minimize limb loss.
